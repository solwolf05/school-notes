### Rationalising the Denominator

Use difference of two squares.

$$
a^2-b^2 = (a+b)(a-b)
$$

Example:

$$
\begin{align}
&\frac{1}{1 + \sqrt{ 2 }} \\
= &\frac{1}{1 + \sqrt{ 2 }} \cdot \frac{1 - \sqrt{ 2 }}{1-\sqrt{ 2 }} \\
= &\frac{1-\sqrt{ 2 }}{-1} \\
= &\sqrt{ 2 } - 1
\end{align}
$$

### Solving Quadratic Equations

#### Monic

Factors of $c$ that add to $b$.

$$
\begin{align}
&x^2 + 7x + 12 = 0 \\
&(x + 3)(x + 4) = 0 \\
&x = -3 \\
&x = -4
\end{align}
$$

#### Non-Monic

Factors of $c + a$ that add to $b$.

$$
\begin{align}
&2x^2 + 7x + 6 = 0 \\
&2x^2 + 4x + 3x + 6 = 0 \\
&2x(x + 2) + 3(x + 2) = 0 \\
&(2x + 3)(x + 2) = 0 \\
&x = -\frac{3}{2} \\
&x = -2
\end{align}
$$
